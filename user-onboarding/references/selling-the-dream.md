# Selling the Dream (Pre-Signup Copy)

How to write copy that gets users excited about signing up — landing pages, marketing pages, and anywhere you're persuading someone to try the product.

## Lead with User Outcomes

Every claim about the product should be reframed as an improvement to the user's life.

**The "You Can Slay from Far Away!" principle:**
- Bad: "This thing is totally the best" (talking about you)
- Good: "You can slay from far away!" (talking about them)

**Test every headline:** Does it describe what the PRODUCT does, or what the USER becomes?

- Bad: "Powerful project management software"
- Good: "Ship projects on time without the chaos"
- Bad: "AI-powered analytics platform"
- Good: "Know exactly which decisions are making you money"

## Generate the Aha Moment Before Signup

Help users understand your product in the context of their own lives BEFORE they sign up. The aha moment is: "Wait, I can [desirable thing] without [current pain]?"

**Use storyboards showing the app's place in their real-world workflow** — not workflows inside the app. Show the app fitting into their day, not their day fitting into the app.

## Address Switching Costs

Users are already solving this problem somehow — even if it's with spreadsheets, sticky notes, or sheer willpower. They have an existing workflow, and switching has real costs: learning curve, data migration, disrupted habits, sunk cost of the old tool.

**Acknowledge their current solution.** Don't pretend it doesn't exist. Instead, show how your product fits into what they already do while removing their specific pain points.

**Lower the switching barrier:**
- Offer importers/migration tools prominently
- Show how familiar workflows map to your product
- Emphasize what they gain, not what they leave behind
- Address the fear directly: "Bring your existing data in one click"

**In copy:** Name the old way, then contrast with the new.
- "Stop copy-pasting between three spreadsheets. See everything in one view."
- "You've been manually tracking invoices. Let them track themselves."

## The Rider/Elephant/Path Framework

From "Switch" by the Heath brothers. Address all three in your copy:

### Direct the Rider (Rational Mind)
Give clear, specific direction. The rational mind needs to know exactly what to do and why it's the right choice.

### Motivate the Elephant (Emotional Mind)
Connect with their struggle. Forge an emotional bond with your solution. Use:
- **Design Persona**: Give your product a consistent personality
- **Faces**: Strategic use of human faces (attention magnets). Spread across pages, don't clump them
- **Emotional language**: Name their frustrations, paint their aspirations

### Shape the Path (Environment)
Make the signup environment support forward progress:
- Remove **Points of Friction**: Confusion, tedious form fields, unnecessary steps
- Remove **Points of Disconnect**: Things that let users drift away (e.g., coupon code fields that send them Googling, external links)
- Minimize cognitive load at every step

## Social Proof & Rational Ammunition

### Testimonials: The Four Horsemen
The strongest testimonials include all four: **Face, Name, Title, Logo.** Each element adds credibility. Anonymous quotes are worth almost nothing.

### Points of Parity vs. Points of Difference
- **Points of Parity**: "We can do everything they can" — match competitor expectations
- **Points of Difference**: "Plus, we can do THIS" — where you exceed

### Impact metrics over vanity metrics
- Bad: "10,000 users trust us"
- Good: "Our users close deals 40% faster"

## Copy Tone Guidelines

- Address the user directly ("you")
- Be specific and concrete, not abstract
- Name the pain they currently experience
- Describe the relief your product provides
- Use active voice
- Keep it conversational — like a knowledgeable friend, not a brochure
