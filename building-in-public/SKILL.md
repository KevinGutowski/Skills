# Building in Public

**Sources/gaps:** [references/sources.md](references/sources.md) — 6 Config talks + Valio Con + Show Your Work! + Dive Club; platform/audience candidates in [references/coverage-gaps.md](references/coverage-gaps.md).

The task: you're a designer/maker launching your own thing — build the audience *while* building the product, validate before you ship, and make it pay as a team of one. (Devtool landing pages/PMF → `devtools` (developer-tool-gtm); OSS promotion → `devtools` (oss-strategy); onboarding flows → `user-onboarding`.)

Work the four stages below in order. Each rule is one line here; full quotes, receipts, and per-speaker texture live in [references/field-notes.md](references/field-notes.md), and the pre-algorithm-era bootstrap corpus (tagged *Valio*) in [references/valio-field-notes.md](references/valio-field-notes.md).

## Stage 1 — Validate before you build

- **Validate at the prototype stage** (Perkins): "you can start working towards product-market fit in the prototyping stage" — the loop runs on Figma prototypes and comment sections, no build required. Receipts: comment-to-launch in **32 days**; ~100k waitlist emails from speculative concept posts.
- **Spreadsheet before code** (Garland, *Valio*) — model the money side first; the math surfaces the real constraints before you dive in.
- **Pre-sell before you make** (Long, *Valio*) — a pre-committed audience forces the finish.
- **Sell the render before the product exists** (Di Genova) — audience response to a 3D render proved demand before any tooling was paid for.
- **Fake what can be faked; never open a ship gap** (Garland, *Valio*) — his invite codes "didn't actually do anything"; the six-month finish-to-launch hiatus was his biggest mistake.
- **Don't count on press** (Garland, *Valio*) — bootstrapped products earn attention through the product, not coverage.
- **The financial-viability line has fallen** (Ouriach; Ridd's label: the niche economy) — production got cheap, distribution didn't; thousand-person problems are now open, so keep scope tight.
- **Audience is the remaining bottleneck** (Goldin): "building software isn't as hard as it used to be, but growing an audience is as hard as it always has been" — the product is no longer the moat; budget effort accordingly.
- **Ship faster than feels ready** (Saran): "more features don't get you closer to product-market fit. Once we started shipping faster, we learned faster."

## Stage 2 — Post: rough, problem-first, findable

- **Rough beats polished** (Perkins; Zhang's corroborating number: a raw team reel got **150× the views** of the polished one): "authentic work-in-progress vibes get so much more engagement than perfectly curated content." Post as **"me," not the faceless "we."**
- **Problem first, then a fast visual solution** (Perkins) — people move when they understand the problem and see the solution demonstrated quickly.
- **Persona × cohort targeting** (Perkins) — a broad persona is too shallow; intersect it with a shared-interest cohort, aim each post at one subcommunity, and trend-jack deliberately.
- **The build's exhaust is the feed** (Ruiz) — debugging recordings, half-working demos, and dev chaff are zero-marginal-cost posts; no separate content production line.
- **Post the live edge, repeatedly** (Ruiz): "you own the now and then you you own the now again" — don't hoard a polished backlog.
- **Daily dispatch** (Kleon) — after the day's real work, share one small process artifact; set a timer so sharing never eats the work.
- **Be findable while getting good; join a scenius** (Kleon) — find the community, notice what's missing, fill that void in public before worrying about monetization.
- **Posting is proof-of-work** (Geoco) — sharing your workflow and tools publicly is product discovery and career discovery at once; public hybrid-skill work can mint a role ("brand engineer") before the org knows it exists.
- **Attribution is context; don't become human spam** (Kleon) — credit like a museum label; be a fan first, an open node, a connector.

## Stage 3 — Run the loop: audience ↔ product

- **Optimize early content for feedback, not downloads** (Perkins) — comments are the early-stage metric; "feedback brings more feedback, more eyeballs, more people to your product."
- **The two-way loop is the point** (Perkins): "the marketing has educated the product and the product is constantly educating the marketing."
- **The embarrassment loop self-corrects the product** (Ruiz) — posting pressure doubles as a quality forcing function; the loop running at the API level.
- **Make everything feel like it emanated from the community** (Zhang, via Yuhki Yamashita) — users are simultaneously the research channel and the marketing channel; narrowcast intimacy over broadcast polish.
- **Design decisions can be marketing artifacts** (Saran) — one opinionated, screenshot-able decision (the jumbo button) can out-market a campaign.
- **Position to where people already are** (Saran) — the one-click-login framing succeeded where password-hygiene preaching failed.
- **Position by stalking your commenters** (Di Genova) — let the audience that actually showed up define the category.
- **Let the community set the price; defect → limited edition** (Di Genova) — sealed-bid auction pricing; the "Fallout Edition" recovery converted **94%** of pre-order backers.
- **Tell the work's story; run the story filter** (Kleon; Flarup, *Valio*): "our work doesn't speak for itself" — problem → work → result; and ask whether the choice would make a great story.

## Stage 4 — Monetize and operate as a company of one

- **Watch profit margin, not revenue** (Vallaure) — solopreneur margins run "usually way above 90%"; don't benchmark against funded-startup revenue narratives.
- **Time is the scarce asset, not funding** (Vallaure) — design the business so "it scales with us and not away from us"; automate the decoupled parts.
- **Own the core** (Vallaure) — no-code is fine as long as you understand what's under the hood.
- **Satellite products** (Vallaure) — one shipped product surfaces adjacent demand; aim for a mini portfolio.
- **Stock and flow; own your turf** (Kleon) — convert daily flow into durable stock on a domain/list you control; platforms create reach, owned surfaces preserve memory and monetization.
- **Teach what you know; monetize with consent** (Kleon) — teaching creates demand rather than giving away the store; collect email with permission and "treat it with respect."
- **Train / Trust / Trade** (Di Genova) — every capability gap: learn it, hire it, or barter it; trade is the bootstrapper's hidden budget.
- **Boundary mechanics are survival infrastructure** (Mielke, Petty — *Valio*) — no late-night email; "more time does not equal better work."
- **Expect the unglamorous ratio** (Sheldon, *Valio*) — it's 98% customer-service Friday nights, not the 2% press glamour.
- **Side projects compound** (Smith) — the value arrives "in the opportunities that it creates"; couple products to durable principles, not a single tool.
- Cadence maxim (Perkins): "Passion will get you started, persistence will get you to the turning point, and patience will keep you going."

## The counterweight voice (Aubrey Johnson, *Valio*)

Hold against this skill's build-for-yourself leanings: building for yourself "sometimes I think feels a little bit lazy"; investors weigh traction > team > product; and the purist consolation is fake — "if the product fails and you didn't raise money you still failed it's still zero."

## Checklist

- [ ] Posting as a person, rough and in-progress — not polished brand content?
- [ ] Each post: problem articulated first, visual solution demonstrated fast, aimed at one persona × cohort?
- [ ] Early metric = comments/feedback, not installs?
- [ ] Validating with prototypes + waitlist before building?
- [ ] One opinionated, screenshot-able design decision that markets itself?
- [ ] Unit economics framed as margin + time, with automation eating the decoupled work?
- [ ] Product coupled to durable principles, not a single tool/platform?
- [ ] Daily flow being converted into durable stock on an owned surface?
- [ ] Influences credited with enough context and links for someone else to follow the trail?
- [ ] Audience/community work includes listening, connecting, and teaching — not only posting your own links?

## Relationship to other skills

- **`devtools` (developer-tool-gtm)** — landing-page anatomy, conversion mechanics, and PMF measurement when the product is a devtool (much of its copy/conversion guidance generalizes; this skill owns the audience/content/solo-economics side).
- **`devtools` (oss-strategy)** — the sibling playbook when the artifact is open-source code (READMEs, promotion loops, maintainer economics).
- **`user-onboarding`** — owns the funnel itself (sell the dream, quick wins, share artifacts, ask-after-value); this skill fills the funnel.
- **`hardware-product-design`** — when the indie product is physical: suppliers, tolerances, inventory, unit economics (Work Louder's ops side).
- **`design-prototyping`** — the prototype-stage validation mechanics (what to fake, how to demo); here they double as content.
- **`shape-up`** — appetite/betting once the indie product grows a team.

> **Staleness note (Kevin's rule):** auto-captioned 2024–25 talks plus a 2014 book; platform/algorithm specifics (TikTok duets, trend-jacking, newsletter vendors, social-network examples) decay fastest — the feedback-over-downloads, persona×cohort, owned-surface, and margin-over-revenue rules are the durable layer.
