Books credited in the corpus: Blair Enns (*Pricing Creativity*, *The Win Without Pitching Manifesto*), Jonathan Stark (*Hourly Billing Is Nuts*, *Learn Your Lines*), Alan Weiss (*Value-Based Fees*), Michael Bungay Stanier (*The Coaching Habit*), plus Drucker, Jim Rohn, Dan Lok.

**Third school** — Dan Mall, *Pricing Design* (A Book Apart, 2016): an agency owner's value-pricing playbook with a full worked RFP case study (SuperFriendly × Mega Agency). Mall agrees with Do/Stark on the fundamentals (price the customer, hourly is broken, value first) but forks on proposal mechanics and on pricing yourself *down* — forks are marked inline in SKILL.md wherever they occur. Mall's own credits: Dale Furtwengler (*Pricing for Profit*), Ronald J. Baker (*Implementing Value Pricing*), Dan Ariely (*Predictably Irrational*, the Economist decoy experiment), plus Weiss and Enns again. Mike Monteiro's foreword carries the book's thesis: "You are going to make more money from this book than Dan Mall ever will" (Dan Mall, *Pricing Design*, A Book Apart 2016).

**Fourth voice — terms and contracts** — Mike Monteiro, *You're My Favorite Client* (A Book Apart 2014): the engagement-terms layer the other three mostly skip — deposits, the kill fee, completion definitions, the nine estimate factors, bake-off/spec refusal, termination money. Written to the *client*, which makes its scripts double as what a confident studio says across the table. See [engagement-terms.md](engagement-terms.md).
