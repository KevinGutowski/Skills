# UX Writing Sources

Use this as provenance for the `ux-writing` router and its references. It is not a quote bank.

## Core Voice And Microcopy

- Apple WWDC 2024 session 10140, "Add personality to your app through UX writing" — product voice, personality, and voice documentation.
- Apple WWDC 2022 session 10037, "Writing for interfaces" — PACE, interface-writing clarity, tone, and small-screen wording.
- Apple WWDC 2025 session 404, "Make a big impact with small writing changes" — small copy changes, simplification, and screen-level editing.
- Apple WWDC 2017 session 819, "Designing for a Global Audience" — localization-ready writing. Session is no longer available from Apple's main video pages; kept via WWDC Index/archive notes.
- Michael J. Metts and Andy Welfle, *Writing Is Designing* (Rosenfeld Media, 2020) — voice, tone, inclusive language, error-message structure, and content-as-design framing.
- Aarron Walter, *Designing for Emotion* (A Book Apart, 2011) — design persona, delight layer, and incident communication.

## Error Messages And Failure States

- Wix UX error-message rules — practical failure-copy anti-patterns and recovery mechanics.
- Metts and Welfle, *Writing Is Designing*, chapter 4 — Avoid -> Explain -> Resolve; error testing; business case for content quality.
- Laura Kalbag, *Accessibility for Everyone* — form formatting burden belongs to the product, not the user.
- Eric Meyer and Sara Wachter-Boettcher, *Design for Real Life* — stress cases and emotional context around failure.
- Lidwell, Holden, and Butler, *Universal Principles of Design* — slips vs mistakes, accident prevention, and design-error framing.
- Apple WWDC 2017 session 813, "Writing Great Alerts" — alert necessity, title/message/button jobs, and ambiguity checks. Session is treated as archived/lost-session material.
- Paul Stamatiou, "Crypto design challenges" (2021) — cost-of-failure visibility for high-stakes transaction errors.

## Naming And Labels

- Apple WWDC 2026 session 290, "Craft clear names for features and labels in your app" — belongs / sets expectation / works everywhere criteria, Think-Feel-Do exercise, and Apple naming examples.
- Matt D. Smith, Shift Nudge field videos — CTA grammar: action labels should name the user's outcome, not the mechanism.
- Lisa Maria Martin, *Everyday Information Architecture* (A Book Apart, 2019), chapter 3 — category labels, scope, parallel structure, possessives, and no catch-all buckets.
- Jon Yablonski, *Laws of UX*, chapter 4 — working-memory myth around visible navigation labels.
- Allan Peters, *Logos that Last* — noun-metaphor technique used as a naming generation aid.

## Settings Copy

- Linear settings surfaces audit, summarized in `linear-settings-copy/linear-settings-copy-ia.md` — sidebar IA, page archetypes, row anatomy, option labels, action labels, and settings-home education patterns.

## Reference Map

- `ui-voice-and-tone.md`: voice, tone, microcopy, localization-ready writing.
- `error-messages.md`: failure-state copy, alerts, validation, recovery, incident communication.
- `naming-features-and-labels.md`: feature names, labels, button grammar, plan names, setting names.
