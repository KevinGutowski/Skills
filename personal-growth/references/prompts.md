# Personal-growth prompts and session patterns

Use these selectively. Do not turn every conversation into a questionnaire.

## Contents

- Direction session
- Goal repair
- Setback debrief
- Trajectory review
- Ideal-week check
- Example response skeleton

## Direction session

1. What is the decision underneath the feeling of being stuck?
2. What would be observably better in three to six months?
3. Which recent activities energized you, and which depleted you even when they went well?
4. Which values show up in choices you have actually made?
5. What conditions—autonomy, pace, people, structure, stability, money, place—help you do good work?
6. Which definition of success are you no longer sure you want?
7. What are three meaningfully different ways to improve the situation?
8. What is the cheapest experiment that would distinguish among them?

## Goal repair

Rewrite a vague or defect-shaped goal:

| Instead of | Build |
|---|---|
| “Stop being an impostor” | One recurring, low-stakes practice plus a feedback source. |
| “Make more art” | Protected time, a small output, a cadence, and a review date. |
| “Find my purpose” | Two possible directions and one experiment for each. |
| “Become disciplined” | A smaller behavior, a prompt, and an if-then response to the usual obstacle. |

Template:

```text
Wish:
Best outcome:
Main internal obstacle:
If [obstacle/situation], then I will [response]:
Smallest repeatable action:
Prompt/time/place:
Recovery protected:
Review date:
```

## Setback debrief

```text
Facts without judgment:
Story I started telling about myself:
What the result actually revealed:
Skill, support, or condition that was missing:
What remains valuable from the attempt:
Kind and accurate response:
Next decision: persist / modify / pause / stop
```

## Trajectory review

Score with evidence, not intuition alone:

- Learning: what do I now know that I did not know before?
- Capability: what became easier or stronger?
- Feedback: what did informed people or the environment return?
- Energy: what did the work cost before, during, and after?
- Fit: do I want the real daily work, not only its status or outcome?
- Momentum: would another focused trial likely move something?

Then choose:

- **Persist:** keep the direction and cadence.
- **Modify:** change one constraint or method and retest.
- **Pause:** name what must recover or change before a fair test.
- **Stop:** preserve the learning and release the path.

## Ideal-week check

Before increasing output, place:

- sleep, food, movement, and health needs;
- relationships and caregiving;
- preparation and focused execution;
- administrative work;
- play or curiosity with no monetization requirement;
- detachment, relaxation, mastery, and control;
- slack for disruption.

If the plan works only when nothing goes wrong, it is not yet sustainable.
