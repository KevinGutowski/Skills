# Coverage gaps

Do not promote these candidates into stronger rules without added evidence or accepted examples.

## Evidence gaps

- The personal operating profile is grounded in Meg Lewis's public practitioner framework and adjacent motivation research, but the exact profile dimensions have not been independently validated as one instrument.
- More current systematic reviews are needed for long-horizon goal persistence, recovery across non-office work, and self-compassion interventions in varied populations.
- Energy-based reflection needs stronger examples that separate task fit from sleep, illness, disability, unsafe work, caregiving, discrimination, and financial strain.
- The trajectory test is a practitioner heuristic. It needs explicit cases where persistence paid off, stopping was correct, and apparent momentum was misleading.

## Representation gaps

- The practitioner corpus is concentrated in creative careers and entrepreneurship. Add public examples from trades, care work, education, hourly work, public service, later-life transitions, disability, and unemployment.
- Add culturally diverse sources that challenge individualist assumptions about autonomy, identity, family obligation, and career choice.
- Add examples where stability, community, or caregiving is the primary value and ambition is not expressed through career expansion.

## Safety gaps

- Test the boundary between ordinary self-criticism/exhaustion and requests that need clinical, crisis, legal, or workplace-safety support.
- Add reviewed examples for grief, chronic illness, neurodivergence, and disability without converting difference into a productivity problem.
- Do not add exposure-style exercises without qualified clinical sourcing and a clear scope boundary.

## Evaluation gaps

- Run a fresh descriptions-only judge on the positive and nearest-neighbor routing probes in `docs/personal-growth-source-mining-2026-07.md`.
- Forward-test direction, follow-through, setback, and persist/quit requests without leaking the intended workflow.
- Add accepted response exemplars showing concise output, structural-constraint awareness, non-shaming language, and a genuinely small experiment.
- Check whether “purpose,” “burnout,” and “career growth” over-route this skill when the real owner is people management, org influence, learning design, or safety guidance.

## Source maintenance

- Recheck the public Full Time You curriculum before describing its current format, price, or lesson structure.
- Recheck official WOOP, Fogg, Stanford Life Design, self-determination, recovery, self-compassion, and deliberate-practice sources when making current research claims.
- Keep paid Full Time You material outside the skill unless the author grants reuse rights; do not infer exercises from marketing summaries.
