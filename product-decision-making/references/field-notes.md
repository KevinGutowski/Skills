## Product and strategy

- **"How to be Strategic"** — strategy is a credible, coherent, focused set of actions to overcome the biggest hurdle toward a clear objective. Strategic work means aligning on wild success, understanding which problem matters for which group, knowing the ecosystem/competition, and cutting to the 1-3 things that must go spectacularly.
- **"7 Questions to Impress Your Boss" / "Building Products" archive** — product succeeds because it solves a problem for people. The problem and audience should be clear before solutions; the problem should be worth solving; narrow audiences can support intuition, broader audiences need research/data. Good execution gets to believable conclusions fast; bad execution fails slowly or produces no reusable learning.
- **"Chains of Assumptions"** — impact means an observable effect on a defined target. Every specific task rests on an assumption chain from work item to business value. First-principles thinking means rebuilding the chain and testing links rather than accepting assertions.
- **"Changes and Confidence"** — optimization and new-behavior bets require different confidence. Optimizations can be explored through many small experiments; new behavior requires deeper confidence from customer/problem/market understanding.
- **"Love the Problem"** — solution love creates defensiveness because every solution is flawed and partial. Problem love sustains many attempts and keeps nuance visible.
- **"How to Make Things High-Quality"** — quality/time conflict should trigger the upstream question: would we have chosen the feature if we knew the real quality cost? Cut if not; protect if yes.
- **"The Most Operationalized Product Teams"** — product/data operating model: logging foundation, outcome metrics, weekly monitoring, hypotheses from user conversations and segmented data, experiments, data teams focused on insight, roadmaps from those insights.

## Growth and sales

- **"What Excellent Growth Teams See That Others Miss"** — excellent growth teams own the whole journey, remove blockages, maximize learning rate, practice product growth rather than hacks, optimize relationships not activity, compound wins, and diagnose whether the company is structurally ready for growth.
- **"The Art and Excellence of Sales"** — excellent sales can do the customer's job with the product, join the customer's team, fish for no early, read the buyer's organization, reference-check early, combine playbook discipline with rep creativity, and question whether revenue was truly earned.

## AI, data, and product development

- **"What It Actually Takes to Trust AI With Your Data"** — AI is like a brilliant new hire without company context. Trust requires canonical metrics, business-change logs, analytical playbooks, memory of prior investigations, and measured accuracy loops.
- **"The Data Job isn't Dying Because the Trust Problem is Exploding"** — the data role moves up the stack from producing analysis to curating trusted interpretation and institutional memory.
- **"The Death of Product Development"** — AI compresses mock/doc/code loops; teams should spend more time testing whether an idea has legs: do users want it, can AI/prototypes make it real, and can the team polish/productionize it?
- **"The Thing You Are Expert at Will Be Your Career Downfall"** — experts resist AI through automatic comfort, identity attachment, and love of craft. Antidotes: intentional discomfort, identity expansion, and love of many crafts.
- **"Conversational Interfaces"** — chat is obvious because natural language and messaging are familiar, but blank boxes, refinement-by-text, same-mode input/output, poor scope awareness, and shallow personalization create major UX gaps.

## Influence, culture, and management

- **"The biggest reasons I failed to influence"** — influence starts with knowing what the other person cares about and being genuinely helpful on that axis. Keep a ledger of bets, wins, losses, and overrulings to sharpen judgment. Use advisor mindset to change decision frames; use solver mindset to make the desired decision obvious through action.
- **"What company politics actually is"** — most "politics" in competent organizations is unseen shared goals, unspoken assumptions, and poor translation. Disagree by knowing your position, gut-checking, framing all options, digging for root assumptions, and committing.
- **"Culture Changers"** — culture is the beliefs that water team growth. Change culture by identifying the current belief and why it exists, proposing a better belief aimed at the same goal, and repeating the new story until others repeat it.
- **"A User Guide To Working With You"** — user guides clarify values, communication style, trust triggers, strengths, growth areas, feedback preferences, and expectations. Specificity and vulnerability create trust.
- **"Managing Remotely"** — remote work raises the cost of attention and context. Use kindness, frequent chat check-ins, more async docs, explicit airtime norms, and aggressive meeting cancellation.

## Writing

- **"Reader Empathy and Weird People"** — before a memo/update, ask who the readers are, lowest shared context, intended reader outcome, minimum context needed, and whether another format is clearer.
- **"So you want to write better?"** — writing has three jobs: think, feel, connect. The quality bar depends on the job. Writing to connect is judged by the audience; writing to think is judged by clarity gained.
