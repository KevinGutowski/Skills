## Hardin Valley — a narrowed noun list
The shape of a final ~15-noun list (church client): Sunshine, Hills, Valley, Rivers, Heart, Praying Hands, Hands, Open Hands, Corner Stone, Connected Lines, Connected Shapes, Home, Infinity, Tree, Leaf. Note: all drawable things; abstract values already converted.

## City of Eagan — revisions as upgrades; embroidery in anger
A city brand must run "from huge wayfinding water towers all the way down to stamped cement garbage cans."
- **Round 1 feedback:** type must be legible driving past a water tower. Instead of swapping fonts, Peters built a **custom font from the brand-mark geometry** ("an opportunity to improve the logo"), adjusting letter widths so the wordmark centered under the icon.
- **Round 2:** hill lines "visually overwhelming" + concern about stamped-concrete complexity → cropped tighter and simplified the hill lines while keeping the hill legible. "The result of the revision process can be a stronger mark."
- Embroidery was tested at multiple sizes because "it was important that city staff be able to wear embroidered clothing as part of their jobs" — the §5 functionality test run for real.

## Kwikbit — the Frankensteining exception
Client asked to combine sketches 1 and 5. "Combining concepts or 'Frankensteining Ideas,' as it's called in the design business, is usually a horrible idea. In this case, I was able to blend the essence of one sketch with the outer shape of the other." — essence + container, not parts bolted together.
Second decode: client wanted *italic* type ("speed"). "Italic type may say 'speed,' but it definitely does not say technology." Solution: diagonal cuts — a k/w ligature and triangular i-dots echoing the K's angle. "I identified the actual issues that the feedback was looking to address and fixed them without losing the integrity of the logo."

## New South — noun payoff in a commodity category
Billboard companies default to "a simple font or a plain monogram." The noun process surfaced ram, star, sunrise, bell, pedestal, falcon — yielding a sun+eagle mark where the feathers complete the sun's edge (overlapping geometry + visual flow), and "the full shape forms a circle that fits perfectly in small spaces like mobile and social media."

## Evolution audits (the §9 two-list format, verbatim structure)

**Urbane Coffee** — Not working: icon worked into the logotype reduces legibility; awkward kerning; outdated type; descriptive type too light at small sizes; U/Cup too thick vs the word. Working: "The “U” + Coffee Cup idea is solid" (single working item — that's normal). Solution: evolve the U/Cup into a standalone icon with multi-line motion (visual rhythm technique).

**Eagan Police** — Not working: hand-drawn and inconsistent; overly complex; too many colors; very thin linework; stretched type. Working: "official and expected layout for a police badge"; the traditional shape "is iconic and impactful." Solution: apply the approved Eagan geometric rules and custom type; mono-weight everything; wreath → Eagan leaf shape; eight colors → three.

**Rush Creek** — Not working: unnecessary gradients; word spacing tighter than letter spacing; overly illustrative mark; visually unbalanced font; mark didn't clearly read as R/C or a creek. Working: "I liked the idea of integrating a creek into an “R.”"; blue made sense. Solution: overlapping geometry in the R's leg and the creek; shape container to separate the monogram ("Monograms can be confusing in horizontal logos").

## FM — the instant sale; Golden Bear — two-mark systems
**FM:** two-letter names are gold ("ABC, IBM, Nike, and CNN"). Usual 15-option deck; winning mark placed last; "The client's eyes immediately lit up… 'That's it.'" Time saved on revisions went into patterns and mock-ups.
**Golden Bear:** client torn between a GB monogram and a bear-with-house negative-space mark → use both: "clearly identify which mark is the primary brand mark and which is secondary… a technique commonly used with sports teams."

## Epilogg & Landmark
**Epilogg** (online obituaries): cheeky nouns (skulls, gravestones) were cut — "we decided to let the humor come from the copy and we let the logo be simple and timeless." Final: open book + E.
**Landmark** (church): client developed only **one** of the 15 options instead of the usual three — allowed when conviction is total. Mark: cityscape + implied hill + negative-space cross.

## Wits — badge shape containers
Brand mark = W + roof chevron. Six container options derived from the roofline were presented; client rejected three as "too similar to the shape of a bottle cap" and two as "too expected," and loved the one that "integrated and accentuated the roofline while the curves on the sides matched the center shape." Lesson: generate several geometry-derived containers; expect the client to navigate by elimination.
